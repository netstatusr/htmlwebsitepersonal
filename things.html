LLMs and their price on hardware

Large Language Models are a marvel of technology and they have their usecases but the cost of running them on hardware is substantial. Ever since LLMs became popular, the demand for powerful GPUs and specialized hardware has skyrocketed, making them expensive to run at scale. The cost of water used to cool the hardware is also significant. Since AI is so popular now, major hardware manufacturers are shifting focus to make specialized hardware built just to run and train AI models. Leaving the standard consumers behind on stock and price. This effect can be seen most in GPU, RAM and SSD markets. RAM prices have not gone down since Micron decidied to end their Crucial consumer RAM line. NVIDIA the largest GPU manufacturer stated that no new GPUs will be released in 2026, their first time not releasing a new GPU in 30 years. This is because they are focusing on making specialized hardware for AI and LLMs. This is a problem for gamers and standard consumers who rely on new GPU releases to get better performance and features. The price of GPUs has skyrocketed since the demand for AI hardware has increased, making it difficult for gamers and standard consumers to afford new GPUs.

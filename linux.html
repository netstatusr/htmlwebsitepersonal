About Linux

Linus Torvalds

Linux is a free and open-source operating system kernel, which is the core of the Linux operating system. It was made by Linus Torvalds in 1991. During his time at the University of Helsinki, he developed the Linux kernel as a hobby project. Since then, it has been garnering a lot of attention and popularity over the years from various governments, companies, and individuals. It has been used in a wide range of applications, from desktop computers to supercomputers, and has become a popular choice for developers and system administrators. Linux is popular for servers for its stability, security, and flexibility. Linux is widely used in data centers, cloud computing, and embedded systems and is also popular for its community-driven development model, which encourages collaboration and innovation.
